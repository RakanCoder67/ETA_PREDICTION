How it Works

01
Data Collection
Historic Starlink TLE datasets from Space-Track.org are matched with real-time space weather indices (F10.7, Ap, Kp, Dst).
02
Anomaly Detection
Checks latitude, LTAN, and plasma bubble probability to flag crossings of the Equatorial Thermosphere Anomaly high-drag zone.
03
ML Correction
XGBoost models predict drag residuals in RSW coordinates. The correction vector is rotated to ECI and added to the SGP4 position.

Data Citations & Methodology

Our system integrates real-world astrodynamics libraries, satellite observations, and geomagnetic telemetry to compute atmospheric drag adjustments:

  • Orbital Data Sources: Historic and active Starlink Two-Line Element (TLE) datasets are sourced from Space-Track.org.
  • Space Weather Telemetry: Solar Flux ($F_{10.7}$), Geomagnetic Storm Indexes ($Kp$, $Ap$, $Dst$), and GOES X-Ray Flux telemetry are sourced from the NASA OMNIweb Data Explorer and NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center.
  • Methodology: Standard orbit baselines are propagated using the standard SGP4 (Simplified General Perturbations 4) analytical model. XGBoost decision-tree models are trained in the local **RSW (Radial, Along-track, Cross-track) coordinate frame** to predict drag residual drift. The predicted RSW vector is transformed to ECI and added to the SGP4 coordinates to output the corrected satellite position.
